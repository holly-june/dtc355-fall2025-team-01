The Old Apple Tree

The old Apple tree born from humble seeds, became the matriarch of the entire Pacific Northwest apple industry. In 1826, a London dinner party guest playfully slipped apple seeds into Lieutenant Emilius Simpson's waistcoat pocket, asking him to plant them across the world.

He discovered them months later at Fort Vancouver and, with Dr. McLoughlin, planted Washington's first apple tree.

Around 1830, it produced one apple. Nearly 200 years later, it still bears fruit annually, having survived floods, storms, and progress with roots that refuse to die.
