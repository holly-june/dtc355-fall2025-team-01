The Hyperion

World's Tallest Tree

Hyperion, a towering coast redwood in California's Redwood National Park, stands 380 feet tall. This titan is estimated to be 600 to 800 years old.

Hyperion nearly became lumber in the 1970s. Just 100 meters away lies a clearcut wasteland where loggers decimated over 90% of the ancient forest.

Hyperion survived by sheer luck, weeks away from the chainsaw when the land became protected parkland in 1978. The world's tallest tree exists because someone drew a boundary line just in time.
